About Pascal's Triangle & Singmaster's Conjecture

Pascal's Triangle

A triangular array where each number is the sum of the two numbers above it. Used in combinatorics, probability, and algebra.

Singmaster's Conjecture

Asks: Is there a finite limit to how many times a number can appear in Pascal's triangle? Most numbers appear only a few times, but some appear many times.

The Challenge

Use Romasm to calculate large portions of Pascal's triangle and count occurrences of specific numbers, searching for record-breaking frequencies.
